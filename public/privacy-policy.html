Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 2026

GymMate is a privacy-first, local-first fitness app. The short version: your workout data lives on your own device, and we don't run servers that collect it.

What we store

GymMate keeps your data on your device using your browser's local storage (on the web) or your device's local database (on mobile). This includes:

  • Your profile name and biometrics (age, height, weight, goals)
  • Workout programs, training days, and exercises
  • Logged sessions, sets, weights, reps, and personal records

What we don't do

  • We do not sell, rent, or share your personal data.
  • We do not run analytics or advertising trackers.
  • We do not store your workout data on our own servers.

AI features (optional)

If you choose to enable AI features (program generation and exercise photo recognition), you provide your own Google Gemini API key, which is stored locally on your device. When you use these features, the relevant input — your text prompt, or a photo you take — is sent directly from your device to Google's Gemini API under your own key. That data is handled according to Google's API terms. GymMate does not receive or retain a copy.

Google Sign-In (optional)

If you sign in with Google, we receive basic profile information (name and email) to identify your account. We do not access any other Google data.

Your control

Because your data is stored locally, you can remove it at any time by deleting your user profile in the app, clearing demo data, or clearing your browser/app storage.

Contact

GymMate is an independent companion project. For questions, reach out via the project's repository.
